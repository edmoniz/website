It is understandable that I would want to explore this iamge and to create my own interpretation of it. This farm is located in a rural area near Victoria BC addition when I saw recongnized the potential it contained.

My initial intent was to capture as much by exposing the image to the right of the histogram, but not blow out the hightlights. With digital sensors fifty percent of the resolution is taken up by the top twenty percent of the brightness, the right hand side of the histogram.

Once I had the capture and had completed basic raw adjustments decisions were needed on how to finallize the image. Several techniques were tried but I settled on subtractive editing. This techneque is achieved by taking a large brush, moving the exposure to the lowest point and then brushing over the whole image. This effectively covers the image with a dark layer. At his point you take and eraser brush and start removing the black overlay. This needs to be done slowly with constant evaluation of where the iamge is and where it is going. You can try this several times and each time it will be slightly different. Once you have the effect you want stop and make any minor global adjustments that might be needed.
